This is a critical architectural decision for a health tech product. In healthcare software (HIPAA/GDPR compliant), **data integrity and auditability are more important than storage space.**

Here is my recommendation based on compliance standards and SaaS best practices, specifically tailored for your Ghanaian market context.

### 1. The Core Difference: Suspension vs. Archiving

You should treat these as two distinct states with different business logic:

| Feature | **Suspension** (`is_suspended`) | **Archiving** (`is_archived` / `is_deleted`) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trigger** | Non-payment, billing dispute, or temporary administrative hold. | Clinic closure, merger, or end of contract. |
| **Data State** | **Frozen but Accessible.** Data remains intact. | **Hidden & Read-Only.** Data is preserved for legal reasons but removed from active UI. |
| **User Access** | **Blocked.** Users cannot log in or view patient data. | **Restricted.** Only Super Admins or Compliance Officers can access via a special "Archive Viewer." |
| **Reversibility** | **Instant.** Reactivate upon payment. | **Complex.** Requires a formal "Restoration Request" process. |
| **Billing** | Stops new billing, but retains customer record. | Terminates contract completely. |
| **Compliance** | Data must remain available for immediate retrieval if paid. | Data must be retained for 7+ years (Ghana Data Protection Act) but not actively processed. |

#### **Recommendation:**
*   **Suspension** is for **"Paywall"** issues. The clinic still exists, they just can't use it.
*   **Archiving** is for **"End of Life"** issues. The clinic is gone, but you must keep the data for legal audits.

---

### 2. What "Delete" Should Actually Do

**⚠️ Critical Warning:** In a health application, **you should NEVER physically delete a clinic row from the database** if it has ever had patients or encounters.

*   **Why?** If a patient sues for malpractice 3 years later, you need that clinic's records to prove what happened. If you physically deleted the row, you have destroyed evidence. This is a major GDPR/HIPAA violation.
*   **Your Current Code:** Your `soft_delete()` method sets `is_deleted = True`. This is the **correct approach**.

#### **Refined Logic for "Delete" (Permanent Removal):**
Since you mentioned deleting demo clinics:
1.  **Demo Clinics:** Can be physically deleted ONLY if they have **zero** patients and **zero** encounters.
2.  **Real Clinics:** Must always be **Soft Deleted** (`is_deleted = True`). They disappear from the UI, but the row stays in the DB.
